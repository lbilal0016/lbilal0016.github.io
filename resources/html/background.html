Bachelor in Mechanical Engineering

I completed my mechanical engineering education with a focus on automotive engineering. Throughout my studies, I developed a strong interest in electronic control systems and programming. While working with commercial Computer-Aided Engineering (CAE) tools, I began to realize that my true interest was not only in using existing software, but in building my own.

For me, developing a program to solve a simple problem was more valuable than using a commercial software to solve a complex one. This realization marked a turning point in my career path and laid the foundation for my transition into software development, grounded in an engineering mindset and analytical problem-solving approach.

Master in Mechatronics Engineering

During my Master’s studies in Mechatronics Engineering, I was already working as an automotive software developer. As a result, my academic direction was closely aligned with my professional experience in automotive software development.

I leveraged my Master’s program to deepen my expertise in software engineering, expanding my programming skill set with languages such as C++, Python, and Java. Consequently, my graduate studies became strongly centered on software design and development for automotive systems.
