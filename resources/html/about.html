More about me...

My professional background is rooted in engineering discipline and safety-critical systems. With a bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Engineering and a master’s degree in Mechatronics Engineering, I have developed automotive software for advanced driver assistance systems (ADAS) during the past four and a half years, contributing to projects for major manufacturers, including Volkswagen. My work has primarily focused on functional safety–oriented development, where precision, traceability, and reliability are not optional.

Working in safety-critical environments has shaped how I approach software: structured architecture, defensive programming, and a strong awareness of edge cases. I have solid experience in C, C++, Python, MATLAB, and Simulink, and I am comfortable working within rigorous development processes, including requirement analysis, validation, and verification workflows.

I am currently transitioning from safety-critical embedded systems to web and desktop application development, using my background in structured, high-reliability automotive software to my advantage to build modern, user-facing solutions. With solid foundations in Java, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, I approach application development with an engineering mindset that values clean architecture, maintainability, scalability, and thoughtful design. My goal is to create robust, well-structured software that combines technical depth with practical usability.

I see software development as a continuous learning process. With my background, I am confident in my ability to quickly adapt to new technologies, frameworks, and paradigms, while always grounding my work in engineering fundamentals.
